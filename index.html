New Project

Customer Review Sentiment Analysis

    I.Specify the Question

  • Objective: Understand customer sentiment trends over time through online reviews to identify patterns and areas of improvement in customer experience.
  • Keys Questions:
    • How does the volume of customer reviews change over time?
    • What is the sentiment distribution (positive, negative, neutral) across verified and unverified reviews?
    • Are there significant trends or seasonal patterns in customer reviews?
    • How does the sentiment in verified reviews compare with unverified reviews?

Obtain the Data

  • Data-source:
    • Gather data from an e-commerce or review platform, including historical review data from verified and unverified customers.
  • Data Fields:
    • Review Date: The date when the review was posted.
    • Review Text: The content of the review, which will be analyzed for sentiment.
    • Sentiment Score: Pre-calculated score or label (positive, negative, or neutral) using a sentiment analysis model.
  • Data Collection Method:
    • Use APIs from platforms like Amazon, Yelp, or Google Reviews to obtain customer review data. Alternatively, use web scraping methods if an API is unavailable. If sentiment scores aren’t available, use a pre-trained sentiment analysis model to label each review.

Analyze the Data

  • Preprocessing:
    • Clean the review text by removing stop words, punctuation, and perform tokenization.
  • Sentiment Analysis:
    • Use Natural Language Processing (NLP) to analyze the sentiment in each review. Models like VADER (for general text sentiment) or a fine-tuned BERT model can classify sentiments.
    • Label each review as positive, negative, or neutral based on sentiment scores.
  • Data Aggregation:
    • Group reviews by date, type (verified or unverified), and sentiment category to calculate the number of reviews per category over time.
    • Calculate monthly or quarterly aggregates to identify trends and patterns.
  • Visualization:
    • Time Series Plot: Display the number of verified and unverified reviews over time (similar to the image provided) to see growth and decline.
    • Sentiment Distribution: Plot the sentiment breakdown (positive, negative, neutral) over time to understand how customer satisfaction has changed.
    • Comparison Chart: Show sentiment differences between verified and unverified reviews to gauge authenticity and reliability.

Results

  • Summary of Findings:
    • Present insights on customer review trends and sentiment over the specified period. For instance, note any peak times or seasonal spikes in review volume (e.g., holiday season).
    • Highlight the sentiment trends in verified reviews versus unverified reviews.
    • Identify any significant shifts in sentiment that correlate with external events (such as product launches or policy changes).
  • Key Observations:
    • Report on whether verified reviews tend to be more positive or negative than unverified reviews, indicating customer trust or product quality perception.
    • Discuss if there's a pattern in sentiment decline or improvement over the years, which could reflect on product quality, service changes, or market sentiment.
  • Recommendations:
    • Based on sentiment trends, suggest areas for improvement. For example, if negative sentiment peaks around specific times, investigate potential causes.
    • Advise on encouraging more verified reviews if they show a more authentic customer perspective.
  • Visualizations:
    • Visualizations: Include graphs and charts that provide a clear, visual summary of findings, allowing stakeholders to quickly grasp trends and insights.
